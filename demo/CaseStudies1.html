The challenge

Manual vulnerabilities and invisible leaks

The quarry processed hundreds of trucks daily using paper registers, standalone weighbridges, and delayed Excel updates. This created a blind spot where small manipulations quietly added up to major margin loss.

Manual gate registers & paper slips
Decentralized weighbridge operations
Delayed entry in back-office systems

Material theft was driven by two recurring patterns: unauthorized vehicle dispatches and subtle manipulation of weighbridge slips before they were recorded in systems of record. Supervisors had no real-time way to validate the actual weight, route, and billing against what physically left the yard.

Because the stock position was reconciled only at the end of the day or week, shortages surfaced late and were hard to trace back to individual trips. Each manual touchpoint—slip writing, data entry, and gate logging—became a potential opportunity for error or deliberate malpractice.

The solution

Automated weighment with GPS‑locked dispatch

SAZS Apps ERP for Crusher & Quarry introduced a secure, centralized control layer where every tonne is weighed, validated, and tracked from load cell to delivery point with no manual re‑keying of data.

Automated weighbridge integration captures gross, tare, and net weights directly from the load cells and streams them into the ERP in real time. This removes the human element from the weighing process so that slips cannot be overwritten, under‑reported, or reconstructed after the truck has left.

  • Real-time tracking of vehicle entry and exit, with every movement stamped against a specific order, product, and customer.
  • GPS monitoring on outbound trips to verify that the vehicle follows the authorized route and reaches the intended delivery location.
  • Role-based security that restricts who can view, edit, or approve dispatch records, closing the door on unauthorized overrides.
  • Digital verification where every weighment and gate pass is time-stamped, archived, and available in a tamper‑proof audit trail.

Together, these controls convert the weighbridge from a weak point into a secure sensor network, allowing management to trace any disputed invoice, shortage, or claim back to a specific, verified transaction.

Why weighment integration matters

Removing the weakest link: manual weighing

For most quarries, the weighbridge is the point where value is created or lost. Even small mis‑entries across hundreds of trucks can silently erode profitability month after month.

By capturing weights directly from the load cells and pushing them into SAZS Apps ERP, the quarry eliminated the two largest threats to its margins: under‑reported weights that facilitate theft, and fat‑finger errors that distort invoices and stock ledgers. Every tonne invoiced now corresponds to a digitally signed event at the weighbridge.

The same integration powers real‑time dashboards for management, enabling proactive monitoring of anomalies such as unusually low average tonnage per truck, repeated route deviations, or sudden shifts in product mix across dispatches.
